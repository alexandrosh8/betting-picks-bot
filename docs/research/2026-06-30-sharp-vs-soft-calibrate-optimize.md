# Sharp-vs-Soft: Calibrate + Optimize for ROI — Research (2026-06-30)

Research-only study (13-agent workflow, empirical + adversarially verified) on optimizing the EXISTING sharp-vs-soft line-shopping strategy on the merged Betfair-BSP + football-data sample (123k MATCH_ODDS / 114k OU2.5, 2024-07..2025-12). Strictly within strategy — no new predictive model. Truth metric: held-out CLV vs the true Betfair-BSP sharp close (ddof=1 SE + bootstrap ROI CI).

## Executive summary

The sharp-vs-soft edge is REAL-but-MARGINAL and not yet proven: reproduced held-out CLV vs the true Betfair-BSP sharp close is 1X2 +0.0091±0.0131 (ROI -2.51%, n=343) and OU2.5 +0.0076±0.0103 (ROI -1.36%, n=220) — both CLV CIs straddle 0 and both ROI point estimates are NEGATIVE. Six levers were tested on this metric. Only ONE produces a defensible, structurally-justified held-out improvement: an ODDS CEILING (~5.0) on the 1X2 moneyline, which removes a longshot band whose CLV is -0.0868±0.0196 (>4 SE NEGATIVE on the held-out alone, replicating at -0.0858±0.0185 on the full sample) and lifts 1X2 held-out CLV at thr=0.01 to +0.0163±0.0120 (2-SE CI EXCLUDES 0) with ROI +0.97%. Everything else is hygiene or defensive: devig method is statistically inert within the shift family (power≡additive≡differential≡shin≡logit all tied within 1 SE) except that MULTIPLICATIVE is reliably ~6-8 SE worse on 3-way 1X2 and must never be the default; recalibrating the fair_prob is REJECTED (the sharp fair is already calibrated to ECE ~0.0015/0.0028, the leak-safe beta map converges to identity, isotonic OVERFITS and loses -0.26% OOS log-loss); switching the train objective ROI→CLV-significance and pruning multiplicative is correct doctrine but does NOT transfer OOS (8 train cells clear CI>0, ZERO held-out cells do); the anchor source can't be improved (Pinnacle and consensus tie; BSP-as-anchor is a tautology). Sizing (eighth-Kelly) is defensive only and cannot raise ROI. Net: ship the odds ceiling + governance hygiene now, validate the rest on fresh 2026 data, and do NOT oversell — the honest deliverable is loss-avoidance and a cleaner pick set, not a profit guarantee.

## Do NOT do

- DO NOT recalibrate the devigged sharp fair_prob (beta/Platt/isotonic). REJECTED on data: the fair is already calibrated (ECE 0.00154 1X2 / 0.00282 OU2.5; mean_pred==base_rate), the leak-safe beta converges to identity (pooled OOS log-loss gain +0.0005%/-0.0046%), and isotonic OVERFITS thin tail bins (-0.2580% OOS, worse in 13/16 folds). Where the map moves probs materially it is exactly the negative-OOS-gain folds — applying it is actively harmful.
- DO NOT use Betfair BSP as the fair/selection anchor. Anchoring fair on BSP while scoring CLV on BSP is a tautology, and recalibrating to or selecting on the BSP CLOSE injects closing-line information into selection = a beat-the-close model, which doctrine forbids and the repo backtests show LOSES. BSP stays evaluation-only (the close-independence guards in value.py L110-205 exist for exactly this).
- DO NOT build a per-(league,market) threshold or devig table. Per-league cells are noise-dominated and sign-flip across markets (I2 +0.0145 in 1X2 vs -0.0246 in OU2.5; SP1 +0.0145 OU2.5 vs -0.0043 1X2); per-cell 2-SE bands 0.012-0.06 swamp the spread. Even the existing 6-cell tier control is untrustworthy at BSP n.
- DO NOT read 'odds_ratio best on 1X2 / power best on OU2.5' off the TEST sweep and hardcode per-market devig. That is selecting the max over 8 methods on the same held-out set the headline already consumed; the cross-method spread is <1 SE — noise-mining, not signal. Keep devig_by_market empty.
- DO NOT adopt the high-threshold rows because their ROI looks large (1X2 thr=0.03 ROI+26% n=20 ROIci[-42,+95]; OU2.5 thr=0.03 ROI+29.6% n=21). These are n-collapse small-sample mirages and would not survive a fresh holdout.
- DO NOT quote the +0.0163 ceiling CLV (or any other number here) as clean OOS proof. The 2025 holdout is SPENT — read across PRs #156-160 and every sweep this session. Every point estimate is a pre-registerable hypothesis for fresh 2026 data, nothing more. Re-tuning ANY parameter on this split to push these numbers up converts an honest estimate into an overfit one.
- DO NOT size Kelly to the in-sample TRAIN premium stream (+19.47%/bet) — it is ~20x the held-out reality (negative ROI). Maximizing in-sample terminal bankroll recommends f>=0.5, the exact fraction that ruins you once the edge is even mildly overestimated. And DO NOT loosen or remove the 2% per-bet cap — it, not the Kelly fraction, is the binding ruin control (no-cap full-Kelly ruin 86%).
- DO NOT propose or build a new predictive/ML beat-the-close model. Out of strategy and the repo backtests show those lose. Stay strictly within sharp-vs-soft: sharp book prices the fair, bet a softer book that beats it.
- DO NOT treat any of these as ROI-raising optimizations except the odds ceiling. Devig method, recalibration, anchor source, and Kelly fraction are all ~ROI-neutral on the truth metric; selling them as profit levers misrepresents the data. The honest framing is loss-avoidance + cleaner picks + risk-shaping, with one marginal CLV lift from the ceiling.

## Data caveats

SAMPLE/REGIME LIMITS. (1) The entire study rests on ONE regime: the merged Betfair-BSP soccer sample 2024-07..2025-12 (123,382 MATCH_ODDS / 114,433 OVER_UNDER_25 markets). The second operator tar is partially corrupt — NO 2026 data — so the held-out window (kickoff >= 2025-01-01) is effectively 2025 only. No second regime to confirm transfer. (2) The join is CAPTURE-BOUND: only 7,756 of 46,221 football-data 1X2 rows match (83% unmatched), and the matched subset is biased toward liquid Betfair fixtures — OOS here UNDER-represents the live production universe (obscure leagues, low liquidity). (3) Held-out n is small at every viable operating point (1X2 n=343, OU2.5 n=220 at the headline; ~20-25/market at the LIVE VALUE_MIN_EDGE=0.03), so per-bet ROI is noise-dominated and CLV CIs are wide. (4) THE 2025 HOLDOUT IS SPENT: it has been read across PRs #156-160 and every BSP sweep this session — every point estimate quoted (including the +0.0163 ceiling result) is a HYPOTHESIS for a fresh holdout, not clean OOS proof. WHAT BETTER DATA CHANGES: the 2026 BSP block (when the operator supplies an uncorrupted tar) is the single highest-value input — it would (a) give a genuinely fresh, pre-registerable holdout to confirm/kill the odds-ceiling lift and the longshot-band negativity, (b) raise n enough to test the live thr=0.03 regime where nothing is currently separable, and (c) let the Kelly-shrink k=realized/nominal-edge ratio be estimated on clean settled data. Until then, treat the edge as MARGINAL (both headline CLV CIs straddle 0; both headline ROIs negative) and prefer the downside-protective, structurally-justified changes over any point-estimate chase.
